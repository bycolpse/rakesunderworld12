What is Psychological Horror?

Psychological horror is a subgenre of horror that emphasizes the psychological state of characters, using fear, tension, and dread to unsettle the audience. Instead of relying on visual shock or gore, it plays with the mind, making you question what is real and what is imagined. It often involves complex themes like isolation, madness, and the terror of the unknown. The fear comes from within, from the characters’ own minds, making it more personal and often much more disturbing.
